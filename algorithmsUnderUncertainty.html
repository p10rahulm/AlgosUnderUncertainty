Course Description

Tentative topics: Basics of Probability, and various other topics

Intended audience: Graduate students in computer science and mathematics with theoretical interests. Interested undergraduate students with very strong mathematical skills.

Prerequisites: Mathematical maturity and a solid background in math (probability, algorithms, elementary combinatorics, graph theory, discrete probability, algebra, calculus) and theoretical computer science (big-O/Omega/Theta, P/NP, basic fundamental algorithms).

Grading: 40% HW, 30% Projects, 30% Final.

Topics from Nikhil's course:

The k-server problem:

We look at the k-server problem and proof that WFA is 2k-1 competitive (based on simpler proof in the paper below).
  1. Weak Adversaries for the k-server problem (by Elias Koutsoupias at FOCS'99).
  2. Background on k-server and work functions (book by Borodin and El-Yaniv).
  3. Other lecture notes on the net.
Content Resolution:
  1. Online Contention Resolution Schemes (by Moran Feldman, Ola Svensson, Rico Zenklusen at SODA'16).
  2. Background on (offline) contention resolution schemes and related material (by Bo Waggoner).
Thompson Sampling:

This is a widely used heuristic in practice for multi-armed bandit problems.
  1. Further optimal regret bounds for thompson sampling. (by Shipra Agrawal, Navin Goyal).
  2. Background and related material (useful set of links from Bartlett for a Berkeley course).
Set Covering:
  1. Set Covering with our eyes closed (by Grandoni et al. at FOCS 2008).
Regret for smooth and strongly convex functions:

The paper below shows T^{1/2} regret:
  1. Bandit Convex Optimization: Towards tight bounds (by Hazan and Levy, Neurips'14).
  2. Lower bound can be found in: On the complexity of bandit and derivative-free stochastic convex optimizationl (by Ohad Shamir'13).
Markov Paging

A polynomial time strategy for page requests arriving from a Markov Process.
  1. Section 5.3.2 in Online Computation and Competitive Analysis: Allan Borodin and Ran El Yaniv's book.
  2. IP over connection-oriented networks and distributional paging. (Lund, C., Phillips, S. and Reingold, N at FOCS'94).
Packing Problems

Faster Packing Covering: Improving the dependence on \epsilon for packing problems.
  1. Nearly-linear time positive LP solver with faster covergence rate. (Allen-Zhu and Orecchia at STOC 2015).
  2. Notes for lectures 3 and 4 (by Blum and Procaccia from course CMU 15-896 - Spring'13).
Regret and Correlated Equilibria
  1. Learning, Regret minimization, and Equilibria (Survey chapter by A. Blum and Y. Mansour).
Online learning on graphs
  1. Online Learning with Feedback Graphs (by Alon, Cesa-Bianchi, Dekel, Koren).
